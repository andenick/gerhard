# COFOG - Classification of Functions of Government
## Complete Taxonomy for Government Expenditure Analysis

**Official Name:** Classification of the Functions of Government
**Standard:** UN Classification (adopted by OECD, Eurostat, IMF)
**Levels:** 2 (Divisions and Groups)
**Purpose:** Functional classification of government expenditure
**Use:** Answering "What does government spend money on?"

**Project:** Gerhard - Fiscal Analysis
**Updated:** October 6, 2025

---

## OVERVIEW

COFOG is the international standard for classifying government expenditure by purpose or function. It answers the question: **What is the government spending money on?**

This is distinct from economic classification (which answers: **What type of expense?** - salaries, goods, transfers, etc.)

---

## STRUCTURE

**Two Levels:**
1. **Level I (Divisions):** 10 main categories
2. **Level II (Groups):** 60+ detailed subcategories

**Usage:**
- OECD countries: Required reporting
- EU countries: Required (Level I from 1995, Level II from 2001)
- Other countries: Encouraged by IMF GFS
- Coverage varies by country

---

## LEVEL I - DIVISIONS (10 Main Categories)

### 01 - GENERAL PUBLIC SERVICES
**Definition:** General administration, regulation, and public service provision
**Typical Share:** 10-15% of total government expenditure

**Includes:**
- Executive and legislative organs
- Financial and fiscal affairs
- External affairs (foreign affairs, economic aid)
- Public debt transactions (interest)
- General services (administrative, statistical)
- Basic research
- R&D related to general public services
- General public services not elsewhere classified

**Examples:**
- Salaries of ministers, legislators, civil servants
- Government buildings (administrative)
- Statistical offices
- Public debt interest payments
- Foreign embassies
- Foreign economic aid

---

### 02 - DEFENCE
**Definition:** Military defense and civil defense
**Typical Share:** 1-5% of GDP (2-15% of expenditure, varies widely)

**Includes:**
- Military defence
- Civil defence
- Foreign military aid
- R&D defence

**Examples:**
- Armed forces (army, navy, air force)
- Military equipment and facilities
- Military personnel salaries and pensions
- Civil defense (disaster preparedness)
- Defense R&D
- Military aid to other countries

**Note:** Dual-use items (police, border security) usually in Public Order (03)

---

### 03 - PUBLIC ORDER AND SAFETY
**Definition:** Police, courts, prisons, fire protection
**Typical Share:** 1-2% of GDP (3-5% of expenditure)

**Includes:**
- Police services
- Fire-protection services
- Law courts
- Prisons
- R&D public order and safety

**Examples:**
- Police forces
- Fire departments
- Court system (judges, courts)
- Prisons and correctional facilities
- Legal aid
- Border security (civilian)

**Note:** Excludes military courts and prisons (in Defence 02)

---

### 04 - ECONOMIC AFFAIRS
**Definition:** Support for economy and economic sectors
**Typical Share:** 3-8% of GDP (10-20% of expenditure)

**Includes:**
- General economic, commercial, and labor affairs
- Agriculture, forestry, fishing, and hunting
- Fuel and energy
- Mining, manufacturing, and construction
- Transport
- Communication
- Other industries
- R&D economic affairs
- Economic affairs not elsewhere classified

**Examples:**
- Road construction and maintenance
- Airports, ports, railways
- Agricultural subsidies
- Energy policy and regulation
- Support for industries
- Labor market programs
- Business regulation

**Note:** Large variation by country (infrastructure-heavy vs service economy)

---

### 05 - ENVIRONMENTAL PROTECTION
**Definition:** Pollution abatement, biodiversity protection
**Typical Share:** 0.5-2% of GDP (1-4% of expenditure)

**Includes:**
- Waste management
- Waste water management
- Pollution abatement
- Protection of biodiversity and landscape
- R&D environmental protection
- Environmental protection not elsewhere classified

**Examples:**
- Waste collection and treatment
- Sewage systems
- Air pollution control
- National parks
- Climate change mitigation
- Environmental regulation

**Note:** Often combined with Housing and Community Amenities (06)

---

### 06 - HOUSING AND COMMUNITY AMENITIES
**Definition:** Housing development, community facilities, water supply
**Typical Share:** 0.5-2% of GDP (1-4% of expenditure)

**Includes:**
- Housing development
- Community development
- Water supply
- Street lighting
- R&D housing and community amenities
- Housing and community amenities not elsewhere classified

**Examples:**
- Social housing
- Urban planning
- Water supply infrastructure
- Street lighting
- Community centers

**Note:** Excludes waste water (in Environmental 05)

---

### 07 - HEALTH
**Definition:** Public health services and health care
**Typical Share:** 5-10% of GDP (15-25% of expenditure)

**Includes:**
- Medical products, appliances and equipment
- Outpatient services
- Hospital services
- Public health services
- R&D health
- Health not elsewhere classified

**Examples:**
- Public hospitals
- Primary care centers
- Medicines and medical equipment (publicly provided)
- Disease prevention programs
- Medical research
- Health insurance (public)

**Note:** Major expenditure category, varies by health system (universal vs insurance-based)

---

### 08 - RECREATION, CULTURE AND RELIGION
**Definition:** Cultural services, sports, broadcasting, religious affairs
**Typical Share:** 0.5-2% of GDP (1-3% of expenditure)

**Includes:**
- Recreational and sporting services
- Cultural services
- Broadcasting and publishing services
- Religious and other community services
- R&D recreation, culture and religion
- Recreation, culture and religion not elsewhere classified

**Examples:**
- Public broadcasting (e.g., BBC, PBS)
- Museums, theaters, libraries
- Sports facilities
- Parks and recreation
- Cultural heritage preservation
- Support for religious organizations

---

### 09 - EDUCATION
**Definition:** All levels of education
**Typical Share:** 4-7% of GDP (10-20% of expenditure)

**Includes:**
- Pre-primary and primary education
- Secondary education
- Post-secondary non-tertiary education
- Tertiary education
- Education not definable by level
- Subsidiary services to education
- R&D education
- Education not elsewhere classified

**Examples:**
- Public schools (all levels)
- Teacher salaries
- School buildings and equipment
- Universities and colleges (public)
- Student financial aid
- School meals
- Education administration

**Note:** Major expenditure category; varies by privatization of education

---

### 10 - SOCIAL PROTECTION
**Definition:** Social security, welfare, income support
**Typical Share:** 10-20% of GDP (30-50% of expenditure)

**Includes:**
- Sickness and disability
- Old age
- Survivors
- Family and children
- Unemployment
- Housing
- Social exclusion not elsewhere classified
- R&D social protection
- Social protection not elsewhere classified

**Examples:**
- Pensions (old age, survivors)
- Disability benefits
- Unemployment insurance
- Family allowances
- Sick pay
- Social assistance
- Housing benefits

**Note:** Largest category in most developed countries

3. **Education:** 10-15%
4. **Economic Affairs:** 10-15%
5. **General Public Services:** 10-15%
6. **Public Order and Safety:** 3-5%

9. **Environmental Protection:** 1-2%

- **Economic Affairs:** Often larger (infrastructure)
- **Social Protection:** Often smaller (less developed welfare state)

## USING COFOG IN ANALYSIS

### Policy Questions COFOG Can Answer:
1. **Spending Priorities:** What does government prioritize?
2. **Efficiency:** How much spent per outcome? (e.g., $/student, $/patient)
3. **Equity:** Who benefits from spending? (distributional analysis)
4. **Comparisons:** How does Country A compare to Country B?
5. **Trends:** How have priorities changed over time?

### Combining COFOG with Economic Classification:
- **COFOG:** What function? (Health, Education, Defence)
- **Economic:** What type? (Salaries, Goods, Transfers, Capital)

**Example:**
- **Health** (COFOG) + **Salaries** (Economic) = Doctor/nurse wages
- **Health** (COFOG) + **Goods** (Economic) = Medical supplies
- **Health** (COFOG) + **Capital** (Economic) = Hospital construction

### Combining COFOG with Revenue Data:
- **Net Fiscal Incidence:** Taxes paid minus benefits received
- **Progressivity:** Do poor pay more in taxes or receive more in benefits?
- **Sectoral Analysis:** Education taxes vs education spending

## LIMITATIONS

### Data Availability
- Not all countries report COFOG
- EU/OECD: Excellent
- Other countries: Varies (often missing)

### Classification Challenges
- Dual-use items (e.g., police vs military)
- Transfers between levels of government
- Multi-purpose programs

### Cross-Country Comparability
- Different social systems (public vs private health, education)
- Different government structures (federal vs unitary)
- Different accounting standards

### Timing
- Data lag (11+ months for official statistics)
- Fiscal year differences across countries

## RELATED CLASSIFICATIONS

### Economic Classification (GFS)
**Purpose:** By type of transaction
**Categories:**
- 21: Compensation of employees
- 22: Use of goods and services
- 24: Interest
- 25: Subsidies
- 26: Grants
- 27: Social benefits
- 28: Other expense
- 31: Net acquisition of nonfinancial assets (capital)

### Institutional Classification
**Purpose:** By level/sector of government
**Categories:**
- Central government
- State government
- Local government
- Social security funds

### Program Classification
**Purpose:** By government program
**Note:** Country-specific, not standardized
