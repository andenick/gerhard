Duration is the single most important number for understanding the interest-rate risk of
the national debt. It answers two related questions: how much would the market value of the
debt change if rates moved, and how long has the government locked in its current borrowing
costs?

## Three measures of duration

The data distinguishes three related concepts. **Macaulay duration** is the
weighted-average time to receive a bond's cash flows. **Modified duration** rescales this
to give the percentage price change for a one-point move in yield. **Effective duration**
accounts for cash flows that change when rates change. For ordinary Treasuries the three
are close; the differences matter most for callable or option-bearing securities, and the
project tracks all three.

## The issuance trade-off

Treasury's choice of how much to issue at each maturity is a genuine policy decision with
real consequences. **Short-term issuance** (bills) is usually cheaper because the yield
curve normally slopes upward, but it must be rolled over constantly, exposing the budget to
rapid repricing when rates rise. **Long-term issuance** (bonds) locks in a rate for
decades, insulating the budget from rate increases but costing more up front.

After 2022, when interest rates rose rapidly, the consequences of this trade-off became
vivid: the portion of the debt that had been financed short-term repriced quickly, driving
up interest costs, while long-dated debt issued in the low-rate era continued to carry its
original low coupons.

## Why duration is a fiscal variable, not just a market one

Because duration determines how fast higher rates pass through to the interest bill, it
links the [Treasury market](/treasury) directly to the [federal
deficit](/modern/deficits-debt). A longer-duration debt portfolio acts as a buffer, slowing
the budgetary impact of rising rates; a shorter one transmits rate shocks to the budget
almost immediately. Managing duration is, in effect, managing the government's exposure to
its own borrowing market.[cite:treasury_mspd]
