How does the federal government raise money today, where does it go, and how much does it
borrow to cover the gap? This section answers those three questions with current data, and
adds a fourth: who bears the burden.

## The three sides of the budget

Federal finance has three moving parts. **Revenue** comes mainly from the individual
income tax and payroll taxes, with a smaller contribution from the corporate income tax
and excises. **Spending** is dominated by social insurance—Social Security, Medicare,
Medicaid—together with defense and, increasingly, interest on the debt. The **deficit** is
the difference, financed by issuing Treasury securities into the market examined in the
[Treasury section](/treasury).
