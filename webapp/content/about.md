**Gerhard** is a data platform and website on the history and present of American public
finance. It is named for **Gerhard Colm** (1897–1968), a German-American economist whose
work on the federal budget, national accounts, and fiscal policy helped shape the modern
understanding of public finance as an instrument of economic management.

## What this site is

The site has four parts:

- **[The History](/history)** — a chronological account of American public finance from the
  founding to the present, in seven eras, each illustrated with the data behind it.
- **[Modern Public Finance](/modern)** — the current federal fiscal picture: revenue, who
  pays, spending by function, deficits, and debt.
- **[The Treasury Market &amp; Duration](/treasury)** — a deep look at the structure of the
  national debt, drawing on detailed Monthly Statement of the Public Debt data.
- **[The Data Explorer](/explore)** and **[Data Catalog](/data)** — interactive access to
  every underlying series, with downloads in multiple formats.

## Philosophy

Gerhard is built on the conviction that public finance should be **legible**: that anyone
should be able to see how the government raises and spends money, follow the long arc of how
that has changed, and download the underlying numbers to check the story for themselves.
Every chart on this site is backed by data you can take with you.

The platform is self-hosted and open in its data. See [methodology &amp; sources](/methodology)
for how the data is built and where it comes from.
